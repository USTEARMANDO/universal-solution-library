## Abstract (from PDF — verbatim)
We introduce the Universal Field (Φ), a scalar field ontologically prior to spacetime from which space, time, matter, and consciousness emerge. Unlike standard physics fields that exist within pre-existing spacetime, Φ generates spacetime through its dynamics. The field differentiates through three primaries—Plasma (substrate), Coherence (order), and Fluctuation (entropy)—governed by the fundamental constraint C + F = 1, derived from conservation of total potentiality. We establish the Zero Axiom: true nothingness is impossible; all limits approach but never reach a primordial minimum (ε > 0), denoted by the ancient bindu symbol. We prove the constraint mathematically from first principles and demonstrate its dynamical consequences. The framework predicts a five-stage emergence hierarchy (* → 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 3 → 2 → 1 → *) where Quantum Mechanics emerges at Stage 2 and General Relativity at Stage 3 as limiting descriptions of different ontological stages, resolving their apparent incompatibility. Universal Time (UT) is shown to be a scalar quantity with UT = π/8 at equilibrium, while observable time t = UT × C emerges through coherence filtering. Numerical simulations verify the theoretical predictions. This framework unifies physics by recognizing emergence rather than forcing incompatible theories to coexist.
